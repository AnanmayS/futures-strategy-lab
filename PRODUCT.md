# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Casual and beginner futures traders working on a desktop or laptop who want to understand how a simple strategy behaves without learning a professional backtesting platform first. They need a guided workflow, plain-language labels, and visible cause-and-effect between settings, trades, and results.

## Product Purpose

Futures Strategy Lab turns local OHLCV data into an inspectable EMA crossover backtest. Success means a user can upload a valid CSV, configure realistic futures costs, run a test, and confidently trace summary metrics back to chart markers and individual trades.

## Brand Personality

Casual, clear, and reassuring. The product should feel capable without feeling institutional, playful without becoming decorative, and educational without talking down to the user.

## Anti-references

Avoid dense professional trading terminals, neon crypto aesthetics, ornamental finance imagery, unexplained abbreviations, and dashboards fragmented into excessive cards. Keep the interface simple and minimal.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next action obvious.
2. Connect every result to visible evidence.
3. Explain specialist concepts at the point of use.
4. Prefer progressive disclosure over dense controls.
5. Keep visual polish subordinate to trustworthy numbers.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Respect reduced-motion preferences, provide keyboard-visible focus states, use semantic labels and tables, and ensure trade direction or performance never relies on color alone.
